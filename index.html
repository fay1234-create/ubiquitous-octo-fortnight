You meet Finlay at the edge of town. Folks say he once fell into a bog years ago and came out changed — not just his boots, but his scent.

"He stinks everywhere he goes," someone mutters under their breath.

You give Finlay a new deodorant. He applies it carefully.

But while helping him, the spray accidentally hits his eye. He collapses, blinking rapidly, tears streaming.

The town murmurs, "Oh no…"

You try to run. Finlay chases you, his stench growing stronger as your breath huffs. Panic claws at your chest.

You falter, eventually trapped by the overpowering smell. Nausea rises as you collapse.

“It’s his smell,” someone whispers as you crumble to the ground.

You befriend Finlay, accepting his mother's payment. You talk, and your eyes water from the stench.

He smiles nervously, scratching the back of his neck, trying not to offend you with his odor. Yet, you see a flicker of gratitude in his eyes.

Years pass. You live with Finlay. The house smells of the bog, a reminder of his past accident. You spray deodorant every morning, maintaining a fragile peace.

Finlay hums quietly in the corner, unaware of how the scent still lingers. You sigh, eyes watering, but a strange fondness has grown between you.

You accept your fate and continue your life with Finlay, spraying deodorant daily. A quiet companionship blooms, punctuated by the faint bog smell and shared routines.

You run away, driving into a rainbow, singing along to pop songs. Freedom rushes through you, colors flashing across your vision as the bog fades behind.
